Uncovering U.S. Macroeconomic Dynamics: A VAR-Based Analysis of Causality and Economic Shocks

This project examines long-term interactions among U.S. macroeconomic indicators—PCE, M2, INDPRO, and UMCSENT—using Vector Autoregression (VAR). Granger causality, Impulse Response Functions, and Variance Decomposition reveal how shocks propagate across variables, highlighting demand-driven dynamics and feedback between consumption and production. Results show M2 reacts to real-side variables, PCE and INDPRO reinforce each other, while UMCSENT remains mostly independent. Insights support policy design, financial risk management, and business strategy, emphasizing real-time data monitoring for informed economic decisions.

Developed a Power BI dashboard to deliver actionable insights on sales performance, customer satisfaction, and outlet efficiency for a retail business. The dashboard uses structured data modeling and KPI-driven analytics, tracking metrics like average rating, items sold, and total sales.
